THREATS TO MARINE LIFE

Plastic waste, chemical pollution, and overfishing are some of the biggest dangers facing our oceans today. Millions of animals are harmed by plastic debris, coral reefs are stressed by rising temperatures, and fish populations are shrinking at alarming rates. Coastal development and industrial runoff add more pressure, damaging fragile habitats like mangroves and seagrass beds. These issues are connected, creating a chain reaction that affects biodiversity, food security, and the health of the planet. Our oceans are changing faster than ever, and the impacts are being felt underwater and in human communities that depend on them.

Plastic pollution, overfishing, climate change, and habitat loss are the biggest problems hurting marine life.
- Plastic kills animals and pollutes habitats.
- Overfishing removes too many fish and breaks food chains.
- Climate change warms oceans and damages coral.
- Coastal development destroys breeding areas and feeding zones.
These threats are all connected and make it harder for marine ecosystems to recover.
